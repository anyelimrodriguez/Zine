As you scroll, be prepared to learn more about the LGBTQ+ community in Latin America

The last few weeks spent we spent watching documentaries from around the world to learn more about the LGBTQ+ experience outside of the United States. I decided to continue with this theme for my Zine and give visibility to countries we didn't have time to learn about.

Mexico

The visible center of the LGBT community in México is the Zona Rosa, where over 50 gay bars and dance clubs exist! In 1979, the country's first LGBT Pride Parade, also known as LGBT Pride March, was held and attended by over one thousand people in Mexico City. Ever since, it has been held every June without interruption under different slogans with the aim of bringing visibility to sexual minorities, encouraging consciousness about AIDS and HIV, denouncing homophobia, and demanding the creation of public policies such as the recognition of same-sex civil unions and same-sex marriages and the legalization of LGBT adoption, among others.

Costa Rica

Did you know almost all countries in Central America ban same-sex marriage? Costa Rica became the first country in Central America to make same-sex marriage legal in 2020. Costa Rica is probably the most gay-popular tourist destination in Central America, with San José and Quepos being the most popular destinations for the LGBT community.

Argentina

Argentina is one of the leading countries in South America in regards to LGBTQ+ rights. Same-sex marriage has been legal since 2010. Transgender people have a law reserving 1% of Argentina's public sector jobs.

Chile

Chile just recently legalized same-sex marriage in Dec 2021! On 9 December 2021, the law was approved and same-sex couples will be able to marry and adopt, starting approx. March 2022. In June 1999, The first March for Sexual Diversity was held in Santiago. Since 2001, Gay pride events have been held each year, concentrated mainly in the capital city.
